Chapter 7 DISLOCATIONS & STRENGTHENING
Dislocation Motion & Slip
Slip
The process by which plastic deformation occurs by dislocation motion. Dislocations move along slip planes in slip directions.
Slip Plane
The crystallographic plane along which the dislocation line traverses. Most densely packed plane.
Slip System
Consists of the most densely packed crystallographic plane + within the plane, the most closely packed direction. Varies by crystal structure.
Dislocation Motion
Easier in metals (many slip systems), harder in ceramics. FCC & BCC = ductile. HCP = normally brittle (fewer slip systems).
Edge vs Screw Dislocation
Edge Dislocation
Direction of motion is parallel to the stress direction. The extra half-plane of atoms ends at the dislocation line.
Screw Dislocation
Direction of motion is perpendicular to the stress direction. Atomic planes form a helical ramp around the dislocation line.

3 Strengthening Mechanisms
  1. 1
    Grain Size Reduction вАФ Smaller grains = more grain boundaries = more obstacles to dislocation motion = stronger material. Hall-Petch equation governs this.
  2. 2
    Solid Solution Strengthening вАФ Adding impurity atoms (substitutional or interstitial) distorts the lattice and impedes dislocation motion.
  3. 3
    Strain Hardening (Cold Working) вАФ Plastic deformation creates new dislocations; dislocations impede each other вЖТ strength and hardness increase, ductility decreases.
Cold Working effects: вЖС Yield Strength, вЖС Tensile Strength, вЖС Hardness, вЖУ Ductility (%EL or %AR). Dislocation density increases.

Annealing вАФ 3 Stages
1

Fatigue
Definition
Failure under applied cyclic stress. Repeated small stresses cause slow crack growth leading to fracture. Can occur at stresses well below the yield strength.
3 Factors that Affect Fatigue: (1) Maximum stress in tension, (2) Fluctuation in applied stress (stress amplitude), (3) Number of cycles

S-N Curve: As mean stress DECREASES, the allowable stress amplitude for a given fatigue is INCREASED. Materials with a fatigue limit show a plateau on the S-N curve (ferrous alloys). Non-ferrous alloys (aluminum) have NO fatigue limit вАФ they will eventually fail at any stress.
3 Steps of Fatigue Failure
  1. 1
    Crack Initiation вАФ Small crack forms at regions of high stress concentration (surface defects, notches, scratches)
  2. 2
    Crack Propagation вАФ Crack advances incrementally with each stress cycle. Creates characteristic "beach marks" or striations
  3. 3
    Failure вАФ Occurs rapidly once the advancing crack reaches the critical size (fracture)
